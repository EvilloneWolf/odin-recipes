How to Make Jeera Aloo

This Jeera Aloo recipe is a delicious Indian dish made with potatoes, cumin seeds, and spices. It's a flavorful and easy-to-make side dish that pairs well with rice or bread.

Ingredients

  • 5 to 6 medium-sized potatoes (500 grams)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 3 teaspoons cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 2 to 3 finely chopped green chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (optional)
  • 2 to 3 teaspoons lemon juice (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)

Steps

  1. First, scrub and rinse 5 to 6 medium-sized potatoes (500 grams) in water.
  2. Boil or steam the potatoes along with ½ teaspoon salt in a stove-top pressure cooker, Instant pot, or a pan, until they are tender and cooked well. Do not overcook or they’ll become mushy!
  3. For the stovetop pressure cooker, wait until all the pressure falls in the cooker and then remove the lid. For the Instant Pot, do a quick pressure release (QPR) after 5 to 7 minutes.
  4. Once they are cool enough to touch, peel the skins and chop the potatoes into bite-sized cubes.
  5. Now it’s time to make the jeera aloo. Firstly, heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  6. Reduce the heat and to the hot oil add 3 teaspoons cumin seeds (jeera) and let them crackle for a few seconds as you continuously stir.
  7. Once the cumin seeds crackle and change color, then add 2 to 3 finely chopped green chilies (1 to 1.5 teaspoons finely chopped green chillies or serrano peppers).
  8. Stir well to combine the chilies and cumin seeds.
  9. Next, add the boiled potato cubes to the pan.
  10. Mix together so that the potato cubes are evenly coated with the cracked cumin seeds and green chilies.
  11. Sprinkle the potatoes with ½ teaspoon of turmeric powder, ½ teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste.
  12. Sauté the potatoes for 2 to 3 minutes on low to medium-low heat, stirring often to keep the potatoes from sticking and overcooking.
  13. Next, add 2 to 3 teaspoons of lemon juice, depending on how much sour flavor you like. Start with 1 teaspoon and taste as you add more to reach the desired level of tanginess.
  14. Lastly, add 1/4 cup of chopped coriander (cilantro) leaves. Gently mix the coriander into the jeera aloo, and turn off the heat.

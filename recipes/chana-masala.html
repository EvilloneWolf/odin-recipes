How to Make Chana Masala

This simple Chana Masala recipe is a flavorful and hearty Indian dish made with chickpeas and a blend of aromatic spices. It's a classic vegetarian dish that's perfect for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried white chickpeas (chana or chole)
  • 3 cups of water for soaking
  • 2 to 3 dried amla pieces or 1 black tea bag
  • 2.5 to 3 cups of water for cooking
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt for cooking chickpeas
  • For Chole Masala Powder:
    • 2 black cardamoms
    • 1-inch cinnamon
    • 3 to 4 peppercorns
    • 2 cloves
    • 1 medium tej patta (Indian bay leaf) or 2 small tej patta
    • 1/4 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
    • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon dry pomegranate seeds (optional)
    • 1 to 2 dry red chilies
  • 1.5 to 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/3 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • 2 to 3 slit green chilies
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 to 1.25 cups of reserved stock or water
  • 1 teaspoon amchur powder (dry mango powder) or 1/2 to 1 teaspoon lemon juice (if not using dry pomegranate seeds)
  • Chopped onions and cilantro (coriander leaves) for garnish

Steps

  1. Rinse 1 cup dried white chickpeas (chana or chole) in fresh water a couple of times. Then soak them overnight or for 8 to 9 hours in 3 cups of water.
  2. After soaking, drain the water and rinse the soaked chickpeas in fresh water for a couple of times.
  3. In a 3-liter stovetop pressure cooker, add the chickpeas along with 2 to 3 dried amla pieces or 1 black tea bag. Add 2.5 to 3 cups of water and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Pressure cook the chickpeas for 12 to 15 whistles on medium heat until they are well-cooked and soft enough to mash with a spoon.
  4. Meanwhile, add all the whole spices for the chole masala to a pan and roast them on low heat until they become extra browned. Let them cool and then grind them finely in a spice grinder.
  5. Heat 1.5 to 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan or kadai. Add 1/2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste and sauté briefly until the raw aroma disappears.
  6. Add 1/3 cup finely chopped onions and sauté until they soften and turn translucent or light brown.
  7. Add 1/2 cup finely chopped tomatoes and sauté until they soften and the oil starts to leave the sides of the mixture.
  8. Reduce the heat and add the powdered spices along with 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder, 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder, and 1/4 teaspoon garam masala (optional). Mix well.
  9. Add 2 to 3 slit green chilies and the cooked and drained chickpeas. Stir well.
  10. Season with salt and add about 1 to 1.25 cups of reserved stock or water. Stir well.
  11. For a thinner gravy, cover and simmer on low to medium heat. To thicken the gravy, mash some chickpeas with a spoon or reduce the amount of water. Adjust the consistency to your liking.
  12. If you didn't add dry pomegranate seeds while roasting the spices, add 1 teaspoon amchur powder (dry mango powder) or 1/2 to 1 teaspoon lemon juice, according to your taste. Stir well.
  13. Chana Masala is ready to be served. Garnish with chopped onions and cilantro (coriander leaves).
  14. Serve with Indian bread like bhatura or poori, along with a side of sliced onions, ginger julienne, and lime or lemon wedges.
